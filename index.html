About Me

I am Zhen Shen, a Ph.D. candidate in Real Estate Finance and Economics at The University of Hong Kong, supervised by Prof. S.K. Wong. My research focuses on how National High-Tech Zones (HTZs) in China transition from landlord-oriented models to ecosystem orchestrators through "Soft Supply"—a bundle of operational governance, specialized services, and relational networks.

My doctoral thesis, titled "From Soft Supply to Value Creation: Unpacking the Micro-Mechanisms of Governance, Symbiosis, and Resilience in China's National High-Tech Zones", develops a unified Perception-Mechanism-Outcome framework to explain how zone-level governance inputs are perceived, absorbed, and converted into firm-level economic outcomes. My work integrates resource-based view, social capital theory, organizational support theory, and dynamic capabilities theory.
