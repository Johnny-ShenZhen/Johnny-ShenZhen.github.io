About Me

I am Zhen Shen, a Ph.D. candidate in Real Estate Finance and Economics at The University of Hong Kong, supervised by Prof. S.K. Wong. My doctoral thesis, titled "Redefining High-Tech Zones in China: From Land Leasing to Value-Driven Innovation Ecosystems", develops a unified Perception-Mechanism-Outcome framework to explain how zone-level governance inputs (Soft Supply) are perceived, absorbed, and converted into firm-level economic outcomes through symbiotic partnerships and supply chain resilience.

My research integrates the resource-based view, social capital theory, organizational support theory, and dynamic capabilities theory to understand how National High-Tech Zones (HTZs) in China transition from landlord-oriented models to ecosystem orchestrators.

Research Context

Shanghai Zizhu National High-Tech Park — My primary case study site. Zizhu is a leading science park in China, home to over 6,100 enterprises and 150,000 technology professionals. It serves as the empirical foundation for Studies 1 and 2 of my dissertation, where I investigate how stakeholders perceive park attributes and how symbiotic partnerships between park management and tenant firms drive innovation and value creation.
